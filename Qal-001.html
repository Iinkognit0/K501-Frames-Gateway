Chapter 2 â€” Formal Frame Definition (Mathematical Model)


At the core of QAL lies the Frame as the minimal archival unit.

A Frame represents a single, immutable record of presence.

2.1 Frame as a Mathematical Object


A QAL Frame is defined as a 7-tuple:


F := \langle id,\ t,\ m,\ ts,\ src,\ h,\ p \rangle


where:

  • id \in \Sigma^{*}

    Unique identifier (string, collision-free by construction).

  • t \in \mathcal{T}

    Frame type, drawn from a finite registry \mathcal{T}.

  • m \in \mathcal{M}

    Mode descriptor (e.g. REFERENCE_ONLY, NO_EXECUTION).

  • ts := \langle utc,\ epoch \rangle

    Temporal anchor:

    • utc \in \text{ISO-8601} \cup \{\text{UNKNOWN}\}

    • epoch \in \mathbb{N} \cup \{\text{UNKNOWN}\}

  • src := \langle origin,\ agent,\ channel,\ scope \rangle

    Provenance tuple (purely descriptive, non-authoritative).

  • h := \langle alg,\ self,\ prev \rangle

    Cryptographic linkage:

    • alg \in \mathcal{H} \cup \{\text{NONE}\}

    • self \in \{0,1\}^{*} \cup \{\text{NONE}\}

    • prev \in \{0,1\}^{*} \cup \{\text{NONE}\}

  • p \in \mathcal{P}

    Payload (opaque byte sequence or structured object).

2.2 Presence Axiom


A Frame asserts only:


\exists F \quad \text{at time } ts


No implication is made regarding:

  • correctness of p

  • truth of p

  • intent of src


Presence is strictly weaker than truth.

2.3 Immutability Rule


For any two Frames F_i, F_j:


id_i = id_j \Rightarrow F_i = F_j


Mutation is forbidden.

Any change must result in a new Frame with a new identifier.
