11.1 Monotonic Extension Principle

Let A be a complete QAL artifact and A' an extension of A.

A \preceq A'

denotes that A' extends A monotonically.

Monotonicity requires that no existing component of A is altered or invalidated in A'.

11.2 Append-Only Constraint

All extensions MUST be append-only.

Formally, if A is represented as an ordered sequence:

A = (a_1, a_2, \dots, a_n)

then any extension satisfies:

A' = (a_1, a_2, \dots, a_n, a_{n+1}, \dots, a_{n+m})

for m \ge 1.

Deletion or modification of any a_i with i \le n is forbidden.

11.3 Preservation of Identity

For every artifact a_i \in A:

\mathrm{HASH}(a_i)_{A'} = \mathrm{HASH}(a_i)_A

Identity is invariant under extension.

11.4 Ordering Stability

The relative order of existing artifacts MUST remain unchanged.

If:

a_i \prec a_j \quad \text{in } A

then:

a_i \prec a_j \quad \text{in } A'

for all valid extensions.

11.5 Time Anchor Compatibility

New artifacts MAY introduce new time anchors, but MUST NOT contradict existing ones.

Let T_i and T_j be time anchors with i < j:

T_i.\mathrm{epoch} \le T_j.\mathrm{epoch}

Violations break monotonicity.
