Addendum — Formal Separation of Proof and Interpretation

For any QAL frame
F := (B, h, t_u, t_{utc}, m),
only the triple (B, h, t_u) participates in mathematical proof.

Metadata m and any derived structure are explicitly excluded from the proof domain.

Formally, the proof domain is defined as:

P(F) := (B, h, t_u)

and the interpretation domain as:

I(F) := (t_{utc}, m, \text{external context})

Correctness of P(F) is decidable via recomputation and comparison alone,
while I(F) remains non-decidable and non-binding.

This separation guarantees that QAL proofs are invariant under
changes in language, presentation, metadata schemas, or interpretative frameworks.
